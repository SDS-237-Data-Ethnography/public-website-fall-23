What does the following statement mean to you?

On April 10, 2023 at 9AM Northampton’s reported AQI was 54.

Turn to a neighbor and discuss:

  • What information do we need to be able to interpret the meaning of the number 54 in the following slide?

What is ‘evidence’?

  • Etymologically dual meaning:
    • Transparent, obvious, clear, speaking for itself
    • Appearance or indication, requiring interpretation

Biruk, Cal. 2018. Cooking Data: Culture and Politics in an African Research World. Illustrated edition. Durham: Duke University Press Books.

How do numbers acquire meaning? Through what means do we determine their significance?

Jot down a number that hold meaning to you and what it represents.

Turn to a neighbor and discuss:

  • How do you know if this is a big or small quantity?
  • Do you have a yardstick to help you determine this?
  • How might this number mean different things to different social groups?

Interpretive Flexibility

  • Anti-determinist frameworks
  • Refers to the differing meanings that social groups assign to technologies
  • Explain how technologies are “socially constructed”

Mobilizing Data Narratives

  • Mobilization refers to the processes by which people prepare something to be put to use or into action
  • Stakeholders strategically engage in meaning-making activities around data
  • Shapes societal interpretations of data

Framing Statistics

  • Comparison/Ranking
    • Is x more/less than y?
  • Standards-Setting/Benchmarking
    • Does x meet z standard/benchmark?
  • Appealing to Emotion/Lived Experience
    • How does x make me feel/relate to my life?

“The actual number of Americans jailed or imprisoned, about 2.3 million.” - Matt Korostoff

Take-aways

  1. The meaning/significance of numbers is not self-evident.
  2. Data becomes meaningful as stakeholders develop tools for narrating the significance of a number.
  3. Ethnographers can study these meaning-making practices.
